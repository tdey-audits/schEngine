\question \textbf{1.} Two poles of height $6$ m and $11$ m stand on a plane ground. If the distance between their feet is $12$ m, find the distance between their tops.

\textbf{Answer:}
(B)

\textbf{Solution:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Let the poles be $AB = 6$ m and $CD = 11$ m. The distance between them is $BD = 12$ m.
  \item Draw a line from $A$ parallel to $BD$ meeting $CD$ at $E$.
  \item Then $CE = CD - ED = 11 - 6 = 5$ m.
  \item In right $\triangle AEC$, $AC^2 = AE^2 + CE^2 = 12^2 + 5^2 = 144 + 25 = 169$.
  \item $AC = 13$ m.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Derivation:}
\begin{center}
\fitmath{AC = \sqrt{12^2 + (11-6)^2} = \sqrt{144 + 25} = \sqrt{169} = 13}
\end{center}
\hfill \textit{Triangles — Pythagoras Theorem}
